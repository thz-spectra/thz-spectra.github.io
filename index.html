Research Highlight

Superconducting split-ring resonators for high-temperature THz quantum computing

Today’s quantum computers operating at gigahertz frequencies require milliKelvin temperatures and dilution refrigeration as cooling technology. Higher-temperature qubit operation would be advantageous from the perspective of reducing the cost and complexity of the cooling system. We propose a Terahertz-frequency spin-qubit platform

that relies on split-ring resonators (SRRs) for qubit entanglement and control. We present computational designs for SRRs resonators operating at 0.3 THz with quality factors reaching 1.9$\times$10$^4$ – over an order of magnitude higher than previous THz SRRs. We also achieve a 2,300-fold THz magnetic field enhancement with vacuum magnetic field fluctuations reaching 20 $\mu T$. These designs yield a spin-photon coupling frequency up to 2.9 MHz for molecular spin qubits, which enables qubit entanglement and manipulation, establishing a viable platform for high-temperature quantum computing.
